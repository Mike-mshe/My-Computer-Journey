Hello, I'm Mike Mliwa

I am a dedicated Cyber Security Level 6 student at Taita Taveta National Polytechnic. My passion for technology has led me into this field, and I have already built 4 websites including this one. This demonstrates my commitment, skills, and hands-on work. I am determined to become one of the top 1% professionals in ICT, who helps protect and safeguard our evolving digital world.

Educational Background

I started my primary education at Mzwanenyi Primary School in 2005 at Baby class through to class 3. My mother relocated to Nairobi and I was enrolled at Bahati Primary School at class four. I completed my Kenya Primary Education at the same institution in 2014, scoring 325 marks.

I was able to join Mbooni Boys High School for my secondary education. It was at this school I was first exposed to computers and "fell in love" with them. My future looked bright and I remember I had a dream to pursue a Bachelor's course in Computer Science. However, this was short-lived as my mother could not fully support my school fees. In 2017, I was sent home for a fees balance, never to come back again. In 2018, I was able to join AIC Mwatate Secondary, but due to institutional challenges, I was unable to continue with Computer Studies. In 2019, I transferred to Mwanyambo Secondary School for my final year due to personal reasons and advice from close friends. I was able to post a good result in KCSE 2019, a B plain of 60 points, considering the circumstances.

I was on my way to join Moi University to pursue a Bachelor of Education (Science). This never materialized as the Covid pandemic hit hard in terms of financing. I was able to secure employment as a sales representative at a local supplier.

Once I had a grasp of life, I was up and about taking several courses online. Be it Alison, Microsoft Learning, Google Learning, or W3schools, I undertook dozens of online courses, eagerly digesting and fulfilling my desire to get a hold of Computer Technology. But after several years, it was evident that without a clear, structured learning path, it was very hard to achieve something. I needed someone to guide me, and who would that be except a "Teacher". I had to find a way of going back to school, and this time it would not be a University. I preferred a TVET institution. I resigned from my work and was enrolled at Taita Taveta National Polytechnic.
